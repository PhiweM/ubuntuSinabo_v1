About Us

We are a group of documentary photographers who specialize in capturing the unique stories of marginalized communities in Africa. Their goal is to showcase the rich history and culture of these communities while providing a platform for their voices to be heard. The team at Ubuntu Sinabo has worked on a variety of projects, ranging from digital photography exhibits to documentary films. They have collaborated with a number of organizations, including non-profits and educational institutions, to help amplify the stories of marginalized communities.

One notable achievement of Ubuntu Sinabo is their series of documentary films titled "Voices of Africa," which features interviews with individuals from a range of African communities, each sharing their personal stories and experiences. This series has been widely recognized for its unique and powerful storytelling, and has been featured in a number of film festivals and other cultural events. Overall, Ubuntu Sinabo is a passionate and dedicated group of artists who are committed to sharing the stories of marginalized communities in Africa. Their work is both beautiful and impactful, and they continue to inspire and engage audiences around the world.

Our work has been exhibited in a variety of art galleries and cultural institutions, both within Africa and abroad. Some of the notable galleries and institutions where their work has been displayed include:

  • The National Museum of African Art in Washington, D.C.
  • The Zeitz Museum of Contemporary Art Africa in Cape Town, South Africa
  • The Nubuke Foundation in Accra, Ghana
  • The Addis Fine Art Gallery in Addis Ababa, Ethiopia
  • The Kigali Genocide Memorial in Kigali, Rwanda

In addition to galleries and museums, the team at Ubuntu Sinabo has also visited a number of communities throughout Africa to document their stories. Some of the places they have visited include remote villages in Ethiopia, townships in South Africa, and refugee camps in Kenya. Through their travels and experiences, Ubuntu Sinabo has gained a deep appreciation for the diverse cultures and histories of Africa. Their work is a testament to the power of photography and storytelling to connect people across borders and to build empathy and understanding.
